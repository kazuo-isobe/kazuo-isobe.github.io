Research

Our group currently focuses on three main themes, combining fieldwork, experiments, and large-scale data analysis:

Soil microbe-plant interactions sustaining ecosystem stability
Microbes play a crucial role in supporting plant health by decomposing plant litter and facilitating nutrient exchange. Plants provide carbon to microbes through their roots in exchange for nitrogen and phosphorus supplied by microbes. These soil microbe-plant interaction develop the soil environment. This project identifies these interactions across different tree species and investigates how they change under the influence of climate change.

Soil microbe-plant interactions promoting ecosystem succession
Soils are crucial for life on Earth, supporting many important ecosystem services at local scales. However, soils are experiencing degradation worldwide. This project aims to identify how microbes and their interactions with plants promote primary ecosystem succession in primitive ecosystems, with the goal of preventing desertification and enhancing soil fertility.

Phylogenetic framework to understanding microbial biogeography and environmental responses
We explore whether interactions between soil microbes, plants, and the environment are context-dependent or consistent worldwide. This project develops a trait-based framework with a focus on microbial phylogeography to understand these interactions and their outcomes in ecosystem processes, enabling predictive analyses.
