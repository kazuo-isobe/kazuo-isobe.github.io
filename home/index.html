Our group, based at the Institute of Ecology, Peking University, studies the ecology and ecosystem functions of the tiniest yet most diverse and abundant organisms on Earth.

Direction
Microbes are the most diverse and abundant organisms on Earth, playing a crucial role in numerous ecosystem processes, including essential nutrient cycling for plant growth. They are highly sensitive to environmental changes on both regional and global scales. Our group is dedicated to understanding and predicting how soil microbes interact with plants and the environment, how they regulate ecosystem processes, and how they respond to environmental changes. Our research spans from primitive soil ecosystems in deserts to diverse forest ecosystems across various climates.

Projects
Our group currently focuses on three main themes, combining fieldwork, experiments, and large-scale data analysis:
(1) soil microbe-plant interaction sustaining ecosystem stability
(2) soil microbe-plant interaction promoting ecosystem succession
(3) phylogenetic framework to understanding microbial biogeography and environmental responses
